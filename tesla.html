FAQ

From the transmitter connected to the Tesla systems:
  • Speed
  • Speed limits captured by Tesla built-in camera
  • Power
  • G-sensor data
  • Battery level
  • Battery capacity
  • Battery degradation*
  • Battery temperature
  • Remaining range
  • Energy consumption by car systems (total, rear motor, front motor, battery heating, seats heating, climate control, lamps, etc.)*
  • Average energy consumption
  • Turn indicators
  • Blind spot alerts
  • Selected gear
  • Current trip information (time, distance, average speed, etc.)
*Not transmitted directly, calculated from received data.

From the phone:
  • Navigation**
  • Who's calling
  • Track name and artist (if the music is played in Apple Music or Google Music app).
  • Notifications from your calendar, GMail Apple Mail apps, and 11 messengers
**By default, your HUDWAY Drive will use Bluetoooth to transmit arrow-style directions from HUDWAY Drive app on the HUD. Widgets and notifications also use Bluetooth.

Alternatively, you can have the app mirror your phone screen to the HUD. This way, you can see Waze or Google Maps fullscreen. However, since your screen is mirrored 1:1 to the HUD, all incoming calls and notifications will appear on the HUD in the same way they appear on your phone screen.
Heads-up display

Make a preliminary fitting of the device:
  1. Place the device on the dashboard directly in front of you. Make sure that the center of the device matches the center of the steering column and the edge of the device matches the edge of the dashboard.
  2. Make sure that the display reflected on the combiner is located strictly in the center and there is no distortion left or right.
  3. Mark the edge points of the device on the dashboard in any convenient way.

Install the device:
  1. Peel off the protective layer of adhesive surface.
  2. Place the edges of the device on the marked points and stick the device pressing it against the dashboard.

Power cable and adapter

  1. Connect the power cable to the device.
  2. Route the power cable from the device to the cigarette lighter socket along the joints of the interior panels.
  3. Tuck the power cable into the joints.
  4. Fix the cable with adhesive clips.
  5. Connect the cable to the power adapter, and then plug the power adapter into the cigarette lighter socket.

Transmitter

The transmitter connects to the vehicle CAN bus and then transfers data via BLE to the heads-up display. Please note that transmitter installation steps will depend on the Tesla model. Detailed installation guides with pictures are provided in our Help Centre:
  • Model 3 (2018-2020)
  • Model 3 (2021+)
  • Model Y
Your HUDWAY Drive device will not work without your smartphone and HUDWAY Drive app.

You'll need the app to find HUDWAY Drive and connect to it, as well as to set up what to display on the HUD and for the navigatoon to work.

The Drive has no GPS modules, which is why it's the HUDWAY Drive app monitoring your location using your powerful phone electronics. And it's the app that listens to (after you've allowed it) which push notifications are coming and who's calling you and then sends this data to the Drive.

Also, it's the app that knows when the transmitter is connected to unlock car-related widgets (for example, battery level, power, or selected gear).
